How to Choose a Football Jersey

Replica vs Authentic

Replica (stadium) jerseys are the fan version — a relaxed, comfortable cut in standard performance fabric with printed or stitched badges, and the better value for everyday wear. Authentic (match/player) jerseys are exactly what players wear: a slim athletic fit, lighter bonded fabric with laser-cut ventilation, and a higher price. Buy authentic only if you'll play in it or collect the exact spec.

Getting the Fit Right

Replica jerseys fit true to size with a relaxed cut, so order your normal size. Authentic player jerseys run slim and athletic — size up one if you want a comfortable everyday fit, or stick to true size for the tight, on-pitch look. Training jerseys like the Tiro sit between the two.

Caring for Your Jersey

Turn it inside out, wash cold on a gentle cycle with mild detergent, and skip fabric softener — it clogs the wicking fibres and can crack printed names and numbers. Never tumble dry or iron over badges; hang to air dry. Done right, both the fabric performance and any official lettering will last for seasons.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between replica and authentic jerseys?

Replica (stadium) jerseys are the fan version — a looser, comfortable cut in standard Dri-FIT or AEROREADY fabric, with printed or stitched badges. Authentic (match/player) jerseys are exactly what players wear: a slimmer athletic fit, lighter performance fabric with bonded seams and laser-cut ventilation, and a higher price. Replicas are better for everyday wear; authentics for playing or collectors.

Should I buy a football jersey true to size?

Replica jerseys generally fit true to size with a relaxed cut, so order your normal size. Authentic player jerseys run slim and athletic — many people size up one for a comfortable everyday fit. If you want the tight, on-pitch look, stick to your true size in an authentic. Training jerseys sit in between.

How do I wash a football jersey without ruining it?

Turn the jersey inside out, wash cold on a gentle cycle with mild detergent, and avoid fabric softener — it clogs the moisture-wicking fibres and can crack printed names and numbers. Never tumble dry or iron directly over badges and prints; hang to air dry. These steps protect both the fabric performance and any official lettering.

Are official jerseys worth the extra money?

For everyday fans, a replica delivers the look and licensed badges at a fraction of the cost. The authentic premium pays off if you actually play in it — the lighter fabric, athletic fit, and ventilation genuinely perform — or if you collect and want the exact match specification. For casual wear, a replica or training jersey is the better value.
