How to Choose Football Socks

Grip Socks vs Team Socks

Traditional team socks are part of the kit and slide freely inside the boot. Grip socks add rubberised sole pads that lock your foot in place, improving stability on sharp turns and reducing friction blisters — the reason nearly every pro now wears them. If you play competitively, grip socks are the single best comfort and performance upgrade for the money.

How to Wear Grip Socks

Wear the grip sock directly on your foot, then pull your team sock over the top to stay kit-compliant. Most players cut the foot off their team sock (or use a footless 'sock sleeve') and join the two with sock tape, so the grip sock provides traction while the team sock keeps the look. Always check your boots still fit comfortably with the extra layer.

Cushioning & Height

Targeted cushioning under the heel and forefoot absorbs impact and cuts soreness over 90 minutes, especially on hard pitches — well worth it for frequent players. Choose over-the-calf height so the sock sits properly over your shin guards, and pick a ribbed, elasticated cuff that grips the calf to stop socks rolling down.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are grip socks and do they actually work?

Grip socks have rubberised pads on the sole that lock your foot to the inside of the boot, reducing internal slipping during sharp turns and sprints. Players report better stability, sharper changes of direction, and fewer blisters from foot movement. They genuinely work, which is why almost every professional now wears them — usually cut down and paired with their team sock over the top.

How do you wear grip socks with team socks?

Wear the grip sock directly on your foot for the anti-slip benefit, then pull your official team sock over the top. Many players cut the foot off their team sock and use sock tape to join the two, so the grip sock provides traction while the team sock keeps the kit compliant. A footless team sock or 'sock sleeve' makes this cleaner.

Are cushioned football socks worth it?

Yes — targeted cushioning under the heel and forefoot absorbs impact and reduces blisters and soreness over 90 minutes, while thinner zones elsewhere keep boots feeling snug. Cushioned socks make a real comfort difference for players who train and play frequently, especially on hard pitches. Just make sure your boots still fit with the extra thickness.

Why do my football socks keep slipping down?

Standard team socks slide because there's nothing anchoring your foot inside the boot. Grip socks solve this with rubberised sole pads. If you stick with team socks, use sock tape or a shin guard sleeve to hold them up, and choose over-the-calf socks with a ribbed, elasticated cuff that grips the calf rather than rolling down.
