Shin guards are mandatory in organised football — and the difference between a routine knock and a serious injury. The right pair balances three things: protection style (slip-in for speed, ankle guards for maximum coverage), shell material (lightweight foam-backed plastic vs impact-absorbing composites), and fit (a guard that's the right length and stays centred on the shin). Beginners and defenders benefit from ankle protection; fast outfield players usually prefer minimal slip-ins held by a sleeve.

How to Choose Shin Guards

Slip-In vs Ankle Guards

Slip-in guards are lightweight shields held in place by a sock or sleeve — preferred by faster outfield players for freedom of movement. Ankle guards add padded side pods that protect the ankle bones and a stirrup to lock them down, making them ideal for youth players and defenders who tackle often. Choose based on how much you value protection versus minimal weight.

Get the Length Right

Measure from just below the knee to about two inches above your boot. The guard should cover most of that span and sit centred on the shin without poking the knee or boot. Brands usually size by player height, so check the chart — an oversized guard shifts around and a small one leaves gaps.

Keeping Them in Place

Modern slip-ins rely on a compression sleeve or a tight grip sock to stay put. Many players combine grip socks with a dedicated shin sleeve, or wrap PRO tape above and below the guard. A snug sleeve is the most comfortable solution and stops mid-game slipping that exposes the shin.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size shin guards do I need?

Measure from just below the knee to roughly two inches above the boot — the guard should cover most of that span without touching either end. Most brands size by height: youth small for under 5'3", medium for 5'3"–5'7", and large for 5'7"–6'1". The guard should sit centred on the shin and not slide around once strapped or sleeved.

Do I need ankle protection on my shin guards?

Ankle guards (with padded side pods) are recommended for younger players and defenders who make a lot of tackles — they protect the vulnerable ankle bones. Outfield players who prioritise speed and freedom usually prefer slip-in guards with no ankle protection. It's a trade-off between maximum protection and minimal weight.

How do I keep slip-in shin guards in place?

Slip-in guards rely on a tight sock or a dedicated shin guard sleeve/stay to hold them. Many players use grip socks plus a sleeve, or wrap the guards with sock tape (PRO tape) above and below. A snug compression sleeve is the most comfortable modern solution and stops the guard sliding during play.

Are shin guards mandatory?

Yes. Under the Laws of the Game, shin guards are compulsory for all players in organised matches and must be covered entirely by the socks. Referees can prevent players without them from taking the field. Always wear them in training too — most contact injuries to the shin happen in practice.
