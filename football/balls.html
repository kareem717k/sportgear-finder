How to Choose a Football

Get the Size Right

Size 5 is the official ball for ages 12 and up, including all adult football. Size 4 is for ages 8–11, and size 3 for under-8s. Futsal uses a special low-bounce size 4. Matching the size to the age group matters — an oversized ball holds back technique and confidence in younger players.

Match Ball vs Training Ball

Thermally bonded match balls are glued rather than stitched, producing a seamless, round shape and a truer flight — but they cost more and wear faster on abrasive surfaces. Stitched and machine-bonded training balls have tougher panels and thicker bladders built for daily abuse. Most players want a training ball for everyday use and a match ball for games.

Care & Pressure

Inflate a size 5 ball to roughly 8.5–15.6 psi (check the print near the valve), and moisten the needle before inserting it to protect the bladder. Keep match balls off concrete to preserve their surface, wipe them down after wet games, and deflate slightly for long-term storage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size football do I need?

Size 5 is the official ball for players aged 12 and up, including all adult football. Size 4 suits ages 8–11, and size 3 is for under-8s. Futsal uses a special low-bounce size 4. Always match the size to the age group — using the wrong size affects technique development for younger players.

What's the difference between a match ball and a training ball?

Match balls are thermally bonded (glued, not stitched) for a perfectly round, seamless flight and consistent feel — but they're pricier and wear faster on rough surfaces. Training balls have tougher stitched or machine-bonded panels and thicker bladders that survive daily abuse on concrete and astro. Buy a training ball for everyday use and a match ball for games.

How much should I inflate a football?

A size 5 ball should be inflated to 8.5–15.6 psi (0.6–1.1 bar), with most players preferring around 9–11 psi for a balanced feel. Check the recommended pressure printed near the valve. Always moisten the needle before inserting it, and deflate slightly for storage to protect the bladder over time.

Why do expensive footballs fly differently?

Premium balls use thermally bonded panels and textured surfaces engineered in wind tunnels to produce a stable, predictable flight with true swerve. Cheaper stitched balls have seams and panel inconsistencies that cause more knuckle and wobble. The difference is most noticeable on long passes, crosses, and powerful strikes.
