Skirts & Dresses Buying Guide

Built-in shorts and ball storage

Every modern tennis skirt has compression shorts underneath — the key detail is whether those shorts have ball pockets. Holding a second ball for serves is essential, so check for thigh pockets or a ball-grip waistband before buying.

Skirt vs dress

Skirts pair with any top and are easier to mix into a rotation; dresses are a one-piece decision with nothing riding up mid-rally. Performance-wise they're equal — buy whichever you'll feel best moving in, because confidence shows up in footwork.

Fabric, length, and movement

Look for stretch polyester or nylon-spandex blends that wick sweat. Pleated backs add motion room without extra length. Standard lengths run 13–15 inches; taller players may want longline cuts for coverage on serves and overheads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do tennis skirts have shorts underneath?

Virtually all of them have built-in compression shorts. The feature to check for is ball pockets on those shorts — without them you have nowhere to keep a second ball while serving.

Where do you keep the ball with a tennis skirt?

In the thigh pocket of the built-in shorts, or tucked under the legband if the shorts have a grippy hem. Some players also use a ball clip on the waistband.

Skirt or dress for tennis — which is better?

Functionally identical. Skirts offer mix-and-match flexibility; dresses guarantee nothing rides up or untucks. It comes down to personal comfort and style.

Can I wear a running skirt for tennis?

If it has secure ball storage, yes. Most running skirts lack thigh pockets sized for a tennis ball, which becomes annoying every single service game.
