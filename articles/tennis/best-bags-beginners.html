Best Tennis Bags for Beginners (2026)

Updated June 2026 · 4 min read · Affiliate links may earn us a commission

You need a bag that fits your racket, has room for balls, shoes, a water bottle, and your phone — without costing more than the racket itself. As a beginner, you don't need a 12-racket tour bag. Here are the best practical options.

Backpack vs Tote vs Sling — What's Best for Beginners?

  • Tennis backpack: Best all-around for beginners. Hands-free, fits most gear, often has dedicated racket sleeves. Easy to carry on public transport.
  • Tote bag: Simplest option. Good if you're walking to a nearby court and just need racket + balls. Limited organization.
  • Sling bag: Compact, stylish, and comfortable. Good for minimalist players. Limited capacity for shoes and extra clothing.
  • 6-racket bag: Overkill for beginners — but if you plan to stick with tennis long-term, a mid-size 6-racket bag provides room to grow.

What Should Your Tennis Bag Have?

  • Racket sleeve or compartment: Protects your racket frame from scratches and impact.
  • Separate shoe compartment: Keeps court shoes away from clean clothes and equipment.
  • Water bottle holder or exterior pocket: Essential for hydration access without rummaging.
  • Key/phone pocket: Small zip pocket for valuables that stays secure during play.
  • Ball clip or pocket: Some bags have a clip or pocket sized for a can of balls — very convenient.

FAQs

How many rackets should my bag hold?

As a beginner, a 2-racket bag is plenty. Most recreational players carry 1–2 rackets. As you progress, having 2 rackets lets you continue playing if a string breaks mid-session. A 6-racket bag is for serious club players who carry multiple string setups or need spares for tournaments.

Do tennis bags work for pickleball?

Yes — paddles are smaller than tennis rackets, so they fit easily in any tennis bag. The organization pockets, shoe compartments, and water bottle holders are equally useful for pickleball. No need to buy a separate pickleball bag.
