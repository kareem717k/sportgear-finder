FAQs

Is the Babolat Pure Drive worth the $200 price?

Yes — if you're playing more than once a week at an intermediate level. It's a long-term investment. A racket you'll use for 3–5 years breaks down to very reasonable cost-per-session. If you're a beginner who might not stick with it, start with the EVO Drive instead.

What's the difference between the Pure Drive and Pure Aero?

Pure Drive: all-around power, slightly more forgiving, suits flat and moderate spin hitters. Pure Aero: aerodynamic frame for faster swing speeds, optimized for heavy topspin, requires better technique. Most intermediate-to-advanced players choose based on their primary groundstroke style.

How often should I replace my racket?

A well-maintained frame can last 5–10 years of regular play. You'll need to replace the strings much more often (1–4x per year depending on play frequency). Only replace the frame if you notice cracks, structural damage, or a noticeable dead feel that fresh strings don't fix.
