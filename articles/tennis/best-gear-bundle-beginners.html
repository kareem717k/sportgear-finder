What You Can Skip

As a beginner, you don't need:

  • A dampener/vibration dampener — purely aesthetic, zero performance impact. Skip it.
  • A 6-racket or 12-racket tour bag — massive overkill. A 2-racket backpack is all you need.
  • Specialty tennis clothing — any athletic clothing works. Court-specific apparel is comfort, not performance.
  • A second racket immediately — wait until your first string break to buy a backup, which will take months as a beginner.
  • A ball machine — useful eventually, but expensive and unnecessary when learning basics.

FAQs

Should I buy a racket set instead of individual pieces?

Pre-packaged "beginner sets" usually bundle a mediocre racket with cheap balls and a flimsy bag. You're usually better off choosing each piece individually — the total cost is similar and the quality of each item is substantially higher. Use our bundle above as a guide.

Do I need new balls right away?

Yes. Old, flat balls bounce inconsistently and make learning proper shot technique harder. Start with a fresh 3-pack and replace when the balls feel noticeably lighter and less bouncy on impact. For regular play, budget $15–25/month for balls.
